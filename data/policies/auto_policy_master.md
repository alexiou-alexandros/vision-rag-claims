# Personal Auto Policy — Master Document

## Preamble

This Personal Auto Policy governs the coverage between the insured ("you")
and the insurer ("we"). It describes the protections available, the
conditions under which they apply, the exclusions that limit them, and the
financial terms (deductibles and limits) that determine what we pay.

Optional endorsements may modify or expand the coverage described here.
Where an endorsement is referenced, the terms of the endorsement supersede
the terms of this master document for matters within its scope. The
following endorsements are commonly attached to this policy:

- Glass Coverage Endorsement (see separate document)
- Tire and Wheel Protection Endorsement (see separate document)

For claim filing procedures and timelines, refer to the Claims Handbook.

## Part I — Comprehensive Coverage

### Section 1.1 — Overview

Comprehensive coverage protects against damage to your covered vehicle
that is not caused by collision with another vehicle or object. This
includes damage from natural events, theft, vandalism, and contact with
animals. Comprehensive coverage is optional unless required by your
lender or lessor.

### Section 1.2 — Covered Perils

Subject to the limits and exclusions stated in this policy, comprehensive
coverage applies to direct and accidental loss to your covered vehicle
caused by:

- Fire, lightning, or explosion
- Theft or larceny, including attempted theft
- Vandalism or malicious mischief, including intentional damage by
  unknown parties
- Riot or civil commotion
- Falling or flying objects, including hail and tree branches
- Windstorm, hurricane, tornado, or other named weather event
- Flood or rising water
- Earthquake
- Contact with a bird or animal
- Breakage of glass, including windshields, side windows, rear windows,
  sunroofs, and exterior mirrors. For enhanced glass-specific terms,
  including reduced or zero deductible options, see the Glass Coverage
  Endorsement, Section 2.

### Section 1.3 — Comprehensive Exclusions

Comprehensive coverage does NOT apply to:

- Mechanical or electrical breakdown not caused by a covered peril
- Normal wear and tear, including paint fading, rust, or corrosion
- Damage from racing or other organized competitive events
- Loss while the vehicle is being used to carry persons or property for
  hire (unless a commercial endorsement is in force)
- Damage caused by radioactive contamination
- Loss of personal belongings inside the vehicle (covered separately
  under renters or homeowners policies)
- Routine tire wear, punctures from road debris during normal driving,
  and sidewall damage from contact with curbs (see Tire and Wheel
  Protection Endorsement, Section 3, for expanded tire coverage)

## Part II — Collision Coverage

### Section 2.1 — Overview

Collision coverage pays for damage to your vehicle resulting from impact
with another vehicle or object, or from your vehicle overturning,
regardless of fault. Collision is typically required if your vehicle is
financed or leased.

### Section 2.2 — Covered Events

Collision coverage applies to:

- Impact between your vehicle and another vehicle, whether moving or
  stationary
- Impact between your vehicle and a stationary object such as a guardrail,
  light pole, tree, fence, building, or curb
- Single-vehicle accidents where the vehicle overturns
- Damage caused by potholes or uneven road surfaces resulting in body
  damage to the vehicle structure (tire damage from potholes is treated
  separately; see Tire and Wheel Protection Endorsement)
- Impact damage including dents, scrapes against barriers, panel
  deformation, and bumper damage resulting from a collision event

### Section 2.3 — Common Damage Types Under Collision

Collision claims most frequently involve:

- Body dents and panel deformation from impact with other vehicles or
  stationary objects
- Scratches and paint transfer resulting from contact with barriers,
  gates, parking structures, or stationary objects
- Bumper damage, including cracks, separations, and complete detachment
- Frame and structural damage from severe impacts
- Damage to lighting assemblies (headlamps, taillamps, fog lamps) from
  front or rear collisions, including broken lamp housings, shattered
  lens covers, and detached assemblies

### Section 2.4 — Collision Exclusions

Collision coverage does NOT apply to:

- Damage caused by hail, falling objects, fire, theft, or other perils
  covered under Part I (Comprehensive Coverage)
- Mechanical failure not resulting from a collision event
- Damage from intentional acts by the insured
- Wear and tear, including paint chips from normal driving conditions
- Damage incurred while the vehicle is being operated by an excluded
  driver listed on the Declarations Page

### Section 2.5 — Fault and Subrogation

If another driver is determined to be at fault for a collision, we will
pay your collision claim subject to your deductible, and we will then
pursue recovery from the at-fault party's insurer through subrogation.

If recovery is successful, your deductible will be refunded to you in
proportion to the amount recovered. For example, if 100% of the claim is
recovered, the full deductible is refunded; if 60% is recovered, 60% of
the deductible is refunded.

## Part III — Deductibles and Limits

### Section 3.1 — Deductible Schedule

| Coverage                              | Standard Deductible        |
|---------------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Comprehensive (general)               | $500                       |
| Comprehensive (glass repair only)     | $0 (deductible waived)     |
| Comprehensive (glass replacement)     | See Glass Endorsement      |
| Collision                             | $1,000                     |
| Tire and Wheel (with endorsement)     | See Tire Endorsement       |

Your actual deductibles are shown on your Declarations Page and may
differ from the standard amounts shown above based on options you have
selected. Lower deductibles are available at higher premium.

### Section 3.2 — How Deductibles Apply

The deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before insurance
benefits apply. For example, if your vehicle sustains $3,000 in covered
collision damage and your collision deductible is $1,000, we will pay
$2,000 and you will be responsible for the remaining $1,000.

Key rules governing deductibles:

- A separate deductible applies to each loss event, even if multiple
  coverages apply to the same event
- When both comprehensive and collision damage occur in a single event
  (for example, a collision followed by a fire), the higher of the two
  deductibles applies, not both
- The deductible is waived when the at-fault party's insurer accepts
  liability and pays the claim directly without subrogation
- The deductible is waived for glass repair (not replacement) under any
  coverage option

### Section 3.3 — Coverage Limits

Coverage limits represent the maximum amount we will pay for a covered
loss under this policy.

**Actual Cash Value (ACV)**: For comprehensive and collision losses, our
maximum payment is the actual cash value of your vehicle immediately
before the loss, less the applicable deductible. ACV reflects depreciation
from the original purchase price based on age, mileage, condition, and
market value of comparable vehicles in your area.

**Repair Cost Cap**: If repair cost exceeds 75% of the vehicle's ACV, we
may declare the vehicle a total loss and pay the ACV rather than authorize
repair. This determination is made by the claims adjuster based on the
estimate from an authorized repair facility.

**Per-Event Limits**: There is no per-event dollar limit beyond ACV.
However, certain endorsements have their own limits (see individual
endorsement documents).

**Aggregate Limits**: There is no aggregate limit on the number of claims
you may file during a policy term. However, frequency and severity of
claims may affect your premium at renewal and your eligibility for
certain discounts.

### Section 3.4 — Diminished Value

We do not pay for diminished value (the reduction in market value of your
vehicle following repair of accident damage) unless required by the law
of your state of residence. Some states require diminished value coverage
for third-party claims. See your state-specific endorsement, if applicable.

### Section 3.5 — Total Loss Settlement

When a vehicle is declared a total loss, settlement is based on ACV at
the time of loss. The settlement includes:

- The vehicle's ACV
- Applicable sales tax in your state of registration
- Registration and title transfer fees
- Less the applicable deductible
- Less the salvage value if you retain the vehicle

If you choose to retain the salvage, the vehicle title will be branded
according to state law (typically "salvage" or "rebuilt"), which will
affect future insurability and resale value.

## Part IV — General Conditions

### Section 4.1 — Your Duties After a Loss

You must:

- Notify us promptly of any loss (see Claims Handbook for timelines)
- Cooperate with our investigation, including providing requested
  documentation and submitting to recorded statements if requested
- Protect the vehicle from further damage following the loss
- Not authorize repairs without our consent, except emergency repairs
  necessary to protect the vehicle from further damage
- Report theft to police promptly and obtain a copy of the police report

### Section 4.2 — Policy Period and Territory

This policy applies only to losses occurring during the policy period
shown on the Declarations Page and only within the United States, its
territories and possessions, Puerto Rico, and Canada. Coverage in Mexico
requires a separate endorsement.

### Section 4.3 — Cancellation and Non-Renewal

This policy may be cancelled by you at any time with written notice. We
may cancel only for specific reasons defined by state law, typically
including non-payment of premium, material misrepresentation in the
application, or revocation of the insured's driver's license.

### Section 4.4 — Fraud

Filing a false claim or providing materially false information to obtain
coverage is insurance fraud and is grounds for immediate policy
cancellation, denial of the claim, and criminal prosecution.
