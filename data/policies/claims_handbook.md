# Claims Handbook

## Overview

This handbook describes how to file a claim under your Personal Auto
Policy, what documentation is required, and the timelines you can expect
for claim resolution. Following the procedures in this handbook ensures
the fastest possible settlement of your claim.

This handbook applies to claims filed under the Master Document and all
attached endorsements (Glass Coverage Endorsement, Tire and Wheel
Protection Endorsement).

---

## Section 1 — Reporting a Claim

You should report a claim **as soon as reasonably possible** after a
loss occurs. Claims may be reported through any of the following channels:

- The mobile app (available 24/7)
- The online claims portal at the insurer's website
- The claims phone line (available 24/7)
- Your agent during business hours

### Reporting Timelines

For damage that is purely cosmetic and does not affect drivability, you
may report the claim within **30 days** of discovery.

For more serious damage, prompt reporting **within 72 hours** is
strongly recommended. Delayed reporting may result in:

- Difficulty establishing the cause of the loss
- Disputes over the extent of damage
- In extreme cases, denial of the claim if the delay materially
  prejudices our ability to investigate

Theft claims must be reported within **24 hours** of discovery.

## Section 2 — Required Documentation (All Claims)

For all claims, you will need to provide:

- Your policy number
- Date, time, and approximate location of the loss
- Description of how the damage occurred (to the best of your knowledge)
- Photographs of all visible damage, taken from multiple angles
- Photographs of the overall vehicle showing the damaged area in context
- Contact information for any other parties or witnesses involved
- Current odometer reading

---

## Section 3 — Additional Documentation by Claim Type

### Collision With Another Vehicle

- Other driver's name, contact information, and insurance information
- Other vehicle's license plate, make, model, and color
- Copy of any information exchanged at the scene
- Police report (mandatory if damage exceeds $2,500 or injuries occurred)

### Theft or Vandalism

- Police report (**mandatory for all theft claims**, and any vandalism
  claim exceeding $1,000 in estimated repair cost)
- List of any personal items that were in the vehicle
- Copy of the keys (or explanation if keys are missing)
- For attempted theft, photographs of forced entry damage

### Glass Damage

- Description of cause if known (rock, debris, vandalism, unknown)
- Location where damage occurred or was discovered
- A police report is **not required** for typical glass damage, even
  if cause is unknown
- For glass damage from vandalism, a police report is required if
  estimated repair exceeds $1,000

### Tire Damage

- Description of the road hazard or event that caused the damage
- Location where damage occurred (street address, mile marker, or GPS
  coordinates)
- Retention of the damaged tire may be requested by the adjuster — do
  not dispose of the damaged tire until the claim is settled
- For damage on private property, a police report is not required

### Animal Collision

- Date, time, and location
- Police report is recommended but not required
- If the animal was a domesticated pet, owner contact information if
  available

### Weather Damage (Hail, Wind, Flood)

- Date and approximate time the weather event occurred
- News reports or weather service confirmation of the event in your area
  (we can typically obtain this independently)
- Photographs of damage taken as soon as possible after the event

## Section 4 — Police Reports

### When Required

A police report is REQUIRED for:

- Any theft or attempted theft (regardless of dollar amount)
- Vandalism with estimated repair cost exceeding $1,000
- Any collision involving injury or death
- Any collision with another vehicle where damage exceeds $2,500
- Hit-and-run incidents
- Any incident in which a driver may have been impaired

### When Recommended (Not Required)

A police report is RECOMMENDED but not strictly required for:

- Single-vehicle accidents on private property
- Animal collisions
- Minor parking lot incidents
- Glass damage of unknown cause
- Tire damage from road hazards

### Fraud Warning

Filing a false claim or providing false information to obtain coverage
is insurance fraud and is grounds for immediate policy cancellation,
denial of the claim, and criminal prosecution. Police reports filed in
support of a claim must accurately reflect the facts of the loss.

## Section 5 — Inspection and Estimate Process

After your claim is reported, an adjuster will be assigned within
**one business day**. The adjuster may handle your claim through one or
more of the following approaches:

### Photo-Only Estimate

For smaller claims with clear documentation (typically under $2,500), the
adjuster may approve the claim based on photographs you submit
electronically, without an in-person inspection.

### Mobile Inspection

For mid-range claims, the adjuster may schedule an in-person inspection
at your home, workplace, or another convenient location.

### Facility Inspection

For larger claims or claims involving structural damage, you will be
directed to a network repair facility for inspection and estimate.

### Direct Repair Authorization

For glass claims and certain other clear-cut losses, the claim may be
authorized for direct repair without any adjuster inspection.

### Standard Timeline

The standard timeline for inspection and estimate completion is **5
business days** from claim report. Glass-only claims often resolve
same-day or next-day. Total loss determinations may require additional
time for valuation research.

---

## Section 6 — Repair Authorization

Once the estimate is approved, you may have repairs performed at:

- **A facility in our preferred network** (recommended; workmanship
  guaranteed for as long as you own the vehicle)
- **An independent shop of your choice** (estimate must be approved in
  advance; workmanship guarantee depends on the shop)
- **A dealer service department** (recommended for newer vehicles still
  under manufacturer warranty, or for vehicles requiring specialized
  repairs)

Payment may be issued directly to the repair facility (preferred for
larger claims) or to you upon completion (preferred for smaller claims
and direct-deposit eligible accounts).

## Section 7 — Resolution Timelines

Typical resolution timelines from the date of claim report:

| Claim Type                                   | Typical Resolution |
|----------------------------------------------|--------------------|
| Glass repair                                 | 1-2 days           |
| Glass replacement                            | 2-5 days           |
| Tire and wheel claim                         | 2-7 days           |
| Minor collision damage (under $2,500)        | 7-14 days          |
| Major collision damage                       | 14-45 days         |
| Total loss settlement                        | 14-30 days from determination |
| Theft (recovered vehicle)                    | Variable           |
| Theft (unrecovered, total loss settlement)   | 30 days after waiting period |

These timelines assume timely submission of all required documentation.
Delays in documentation will extend the resolution timeline accordingly.

For theft claims where the vehicle is not immediately recovered, a
mandatory **30-day waiting period** applies before total loss settlement
can be issued. This waiting period allows time for the vehicle to be
recovered before settlement.

## Section 8 — Payment and Settlement

### Repair Claims

For claims involving repair (not total loss):

- Payment is issued to the repair facility (or to you if you have paid
  the facility directly) upon completion of repairs and verification of
  workmanship
- Supplemental payments may be issued if additional damage is discovered
  during repair and is approved by the adjuster
- Your deductible is paid by you directly to the repair facility

### Total Loss Claims

For total loss settlements:

- Settlement is based on Actual Cash Value (ACV) at time of loss (see
  Master Document, Section 3.3 and 3.5)
- Settlement includes ACV, applicable sales tax, and registration/title
  transfer fees, less the applicable deductible
- If a loan or lease is outstanding, the lienholder is paid first; any
  remaining balance is paid to you
- If the settlement is less than the outstanding loan balance, you are
  responsible for the difference (consider Gap Insurance to avoid this)

## Section 9 — Disputes and Appeals

If you disagree with a claim decision, you have several options:

### Informal Review

Request review by a claims supervisor. There is no formal time limit for
this informal process, but it should be initiated as soon as the dispute
arises.

### Formal Appeal

Submit a formal written appeal within **60 days** of the disputed
decision. The appeal will be reviewed by a senior claims professional
not previously involved in your claim. A written response will be
provided within 30 days of receipt.

### Independent Appraisal

For disputes over the amount of loss (not over coverage), you may
invoke the appraisal clause of your policy. Each party (you and the
insurer) selects an independent appraiser; the two appraisers select an
umpire. A decision agreed to by any two of the three is binding as to
the amount of loss.

### External Remedies

You may also:

- File a complaint with your state's Department of Insurance
- Pursue legal action subject to the terms of your policy, including
  any applicable arbitration clauses and statutes of limitation

The pursuit of any of these options does not waive your right to pursue
others, except that a binding decision under the appraisal clause is
final as to the amount of loss.

## Section 10 — Claim Status and Communication

You will receive claim status updates through your preferred
communication channel (email, text message, or phone). You may check
claim status at any time through:

- The mobile app
- The online claims portal
- Direct contact with your assigned adjuster (contact information
  provided when claim is opened)

The assigned adjuster will provide their direct contact information at
the time the claim is opened. If you cannot reach your adjuster, you
may contact the claims line and request to speak with the adjuster's
supervisor.
