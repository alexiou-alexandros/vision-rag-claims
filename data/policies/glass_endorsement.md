# Glass Coverage Endorsement

## Overview

This optional endorsement modifies the Personal Auto Policy (Master
Document) to provide enhanced coverage for damage to glass components of
your covered vehicle, with reduced or eliminated deductibles compared to
standard comprehensive coverage as described in Part I of the Master
Document.

This endorsement applies only when shown as active on your Declarations
Page. If this endorsement is not active, glass damage is covered under
standard Comprehensive Coverage (see Master Document, Section 1.2) and
the standard comprehensive deductible applies.

---

## Section 1 — Covered Glass Components

Glass coverage under this endorsement applies to:

- Windshield (front)
- Rear window
- All side windows (driver, passenger, rear quarter windows)
- Sunroof and moonroof glass panels
- Exterior mirror glass
- Glass shatter, cracks, chips, and spider-web fracture patterns from any
  cause, including but not limited to road debris, falling objects,
  vandalism, weather events, and contact with animals

Damage to glass components arising from a collision event is covered
under either this endorsement or Collision Coverage (Master Document,
Part II), at the policyholder's option. The lower deductible of the two
applicable coverages will apply.

---

## Section 2 — Repair vs Replacement

Whenever possible, damaged glass will be repaired rather than replaced.

**Repair eligibility criteria:**

- Chips smaller than one inch in diameter
- Cracks shorter than six inches
- Damage not located directly in the driver's primary line of sight
- Damage that has not penetrated both layers of laminated glass

Repairs are performed at **no cost to you**, regardless of which coverage
option you have selected. The deductible is waived in all cases for
qualifying glass repair.

**When replacement is necessary**, the following deductibles apply:

| Glass Coverage Option       | Windshield Replacement | Other Glass Replacement |
|-----------------------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| Zero-Deductible Glass       | $0                     | $0                      |
| Standard Glass Coverage     | $100                   | $250                    |
| No glass endorsement        | $500 (per Master)      | $500 (per Master)       |

The Zero-Deductible Glass option is available in most states for an
additional premium. Check your Declarations Page to confirm which option
is active on your policy.

---

## Section 3 — Authorized Repair Facilities

To receive coverage at the rates shown in Section 2 above, glass repair
or replacement must be performed by an authorized vendor in our network.
You may locate authorized vendors through:

- Your online account at the insurer's website
- The mobile app
- Calling the dedicated glass claims line (available 24/7)

Use of an out-of-network vendor may result in reduced reimbursement,
limited to the prevailing market rate for the repair in your geographic
area. Any difference between the out-of-network vendor's charge and the
prevailing market rate is your responsibility.

---

## Section 4 — Filing a Glass Claim

Glass claims may be filed directly with our glass network partner without
opening a full claim file. This streamlined process:

- Does not require an adjuster inspection for typical damage
- Does not count as a chargeable claim for premium rating purposes
- Can typically be completed within 48 hours of reporting (often same-day
  for mobile repair service)

For glass damage that occurred as part of a larger loss event (such as a
collision or vandalism affecting multiple parts of the vehicle), a full
claim should be filed through standard channels. See the Claims Handbook
for general filing procedures.

---

## Section 5 — Exclusions Specific to This Endorsement

This endorsement does NOT cover:

- Damage to glass that is purely cosmetic and does not affect structural
  integrity or visibility (such as minor surface scratches)
- Damage to non-glass components even when adjacent to glass (such as
  damaged trim, seals, or moldings, unless replacement is necessary to
  complete the glass repair)
- Damage caused by improper installation by an unauthorized vendor
- Damage discovered more than 30 days after the date of loss, unless you
  can demonstrate the damage was not reasonably discoverable earlier

---

## Section 6 — Coverage Limits

There is no dollar limit on glass repair or replacement under this
endorsement. There is no limit on the number of glass claims you may
file during a policy term, and glass claims filed under this endorsement
do not count toward any aggregate claim frequency calculations.
