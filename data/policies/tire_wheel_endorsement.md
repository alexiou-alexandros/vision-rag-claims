# Tire and Wheel Protection Endorsement

## Overview

Standard auto policies provide LIMITED coverage for tire damage. Tires
are generally considered wear items, and damage from normal use is not
covered under the standard Comprehensive or Collision coverages described
in the Master Document.

This optional endorsement expands coverage to include damage from road
hazards that would otherwise be excluded. This endorsement applies only
when shown as active on your Declarations Page.

---

## Section 1 — Tire Damage Under Standard Coverage (Without This Endorsement)

Even without this endorsement, damage to tires is covered under your
standard policy in certain limited circumstances:

- The damage results directly from a covered collision event (see Master
  Document, Section 2.2). For example, tire damage sustained in a
  collision with another vehicle is covered under Collision Coverage.
- The tire is damaged as a result of vandalism, including slashing or
  intentional puncture (covered under Comprehensive Coverage, Master
  Document Section 1.2).
- The tire damage occurs simultaneously with other covered damage to the
  vehicle and is part of a single loss event.

A flat tire alone, without other vehicle damage or a qualifying cause
above, is generally **NOT** a covered loss under standard collision or
comprehensive coverage.

---

## Section 2 — Standard Exclusions (Applied Without This Endorsement)

Without this endorsement, the following tire-related losses are NOT
covered under standard policies:

- Punctures from nails, screws, or other road debris during normal
  driving
- Sidewall damage from contact with curbs or potholes (unless part of a
  collision claim involving the vehicle structure)
- Tread separation due to manufacturing defect (pursue manufacturer
  warranty)
- Wear and aging, including dry rot and loss of tread depth
- Damage from underinflation or overinflation
- Flat tires caused by valve stem failure or other component degradation

---

## Section 3 — Expanded Coverage Under This Endorsement

When this endorsement is active, coverage is expanded to include:

- **Flat tires** caused by road hazards, including nails, glass,
  potholes, debris, and unmarked road obstructions
- **Wheel damage** (bent or cracked rims) from impact with road hazards
- **Repair or replacement of a single damaged tire** without requiring
  replacement of all tires on the vehicle
- **Mounting, balancing, and disposal fees** associated with tire
  replacement at an authorized facility
- **Roadside assistance** for tire-related disablement, including
  delivery of a spare tire or towing to a repair facility

The endorsement applies to flat tires regardless of whether the cause
can be determined, as long as the damage is consistent with road hazard
exposure and is not the result of an excluded cause (Section 5 below).

---

## Section 4 — Deductible and Limits

With the Tire and Wheel Protection endorsement active:

- **Deductible**: $50 per tire/wheel replacement event
- **Frequency limit**: Up to four tire/wheel events per policy term
- **Reimbursement cap**: Limited to original equipment specification
  (OEM) or equivalent quality tires and wheels
- **Run-flat tires**: Covered at parity with conventional tires; no
  surcharge for premium tire categories

Without the endorsement, tire damage covered under collision or
comprehensive coverage is subject to the applicable coverage deductible
($1,000 collision, $500 comprehensive — see Master Document Section 3.1).

---

## Section 5 — Exclusions Under This Endorsement

Even with this endorsement, the following are NOT covered:

- Tire wear and aging, including loss of tread depth below state legal
  minimum
- Damage to tires that were already in poor condition at the time of
  loss (less than 4/32 inch tread depth)
- Damage from off-road use of vehicles not designed for off-road
  operation
- Damage from racing or other organized competitive events
- Cosmetic damage to wheels (scratches, curb rash) that does not affect
  structural integrity or seal between tire and wheel

---

## Section 6 — Single Tire Replacement Policy

When only one tire is damaged and the remaining tires have substantial
remaining tread (greater than 4/32 inch), we will replace only the
damaged tire.

If the remaining tires have less than 4/32 inch tread, we may require
replacement of tires on the same axle (typically two tires) to maintain
vehicle handling and safety. The additional cost of the second tire is
subject to depreciation based on the remaining useful life of the
original tire.

For all-wheel-drive (AWD) and four-wheel-drive (4WD) vehicles, manufacturer
specifications may require replacement of all four tires when one is
damaged, to maintain identical rolling diameter across all wheels. In
such cases, additional tire replacement is covered up to the OEM
specification, subject to depreciation.

---

## Section 7 — Filing a Tire Claim

Tire claims should be filed through standard claim channels (see Claims
Handbook). Required documentation includes:

- Description of the road hazard or incident
- Location of the incident if known
- Photographs of the damaged tire and wheel
- The damaged tire should be retained until the claim is settled, as
  the adjuster may request inspection

For roadside assistance, contact the dedicated roadside line shown on
your insurance card. Roadside assistance is covered separately from the
tire repair/replacement and does not count against the four-event annual
limit.
